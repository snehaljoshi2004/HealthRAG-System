#  Blumenfeld, H. (2010). Neuroanatomy through Clinical Cases (2nd ed.). Sinauer Associates

## Section

Fluent (Wernicke's) aphasia is caused by lesions in Wernicke's area, located in the posterior part of the superior temporal gyrus of the dominant hemisphere. The optic radiations forming Meyer's loop, which transmit information for the contralateral upper visual field, also course through the temporal lobe. Therefore, a single lesion in the dominant temporal lobe can encompass both Wernicke's area and Meyer's loop, explaining the co-occurrence of fluent aphasia and a contralateral homonymous upper quadrantanopia
