# Nazarian, R., Shi, H., Wang, Q., Kong, X., Koya, R. C., Lee, H., Chen, Z., Lee, M. K., Attar, N., Sazegar, H., Chodon, T., Nelson, S. F., McArthur, G., Sosman, J. A., Ribas, A., Lo, R. S. (2010). Melanomas acquire resistance to B-RAF(V600E) inhibition by RTK or N-RAS upregulation. Nature, 468(7326), 973–977.

## Page /

“We conducted whole-exome sequencing of matched pre-treatment and post-relapse tumour biopsies from a patient who initially responded to vemurafenib but subsequently relapsed. We identified a new NRAS^Q61K mutation in the resistant lesion and demonstrated that ectopic expression of NRAS^Q61K induces resistance in melanoma cells. Furthermore, we observed that when RTK expression is elevated—specifically PDGFRβ—the downstream PI3K/AKT pathway is activated, providing an alternative survival signal independent of BRAF. Upregulated PDGFRβ signalling thus bypasses MAPK blockade.”
