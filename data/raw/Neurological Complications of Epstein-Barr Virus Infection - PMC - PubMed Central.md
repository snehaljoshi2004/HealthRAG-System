# Neurological Complications of Epstein-Barr Virus Infection - PMC - PubMed Central.

## Section

Infectious mononucleosis, caused by the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV), is widely recognized for its systemic manifestations, including significant splenomegaly which carries a well-known risk of splenic rupture, particularly following trauma. While less common, EBV infection can also lead to a variety of neurological complications, presenting as acute cerebellar ataxia, encephalitis (which may involve focal deficits like hemiplegia), and cranial neuropathies (such as multiple cranial nerve palsies)
