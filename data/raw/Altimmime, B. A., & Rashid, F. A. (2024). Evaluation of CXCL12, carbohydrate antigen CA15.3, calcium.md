# Altimmime, B. A., & Rashid, F. A. (2024). Evaluation of CXCL12, carbohydrate antigen CA15.3, calcium and parathyroid hormone as potential biomarkers for bone metastasis in patients with breast cancer. World Academy of Sciences Journal, 21.

## Page /

“Bone is a common site for tumor cells to settle in breast cancer. It has been reported that ∼70% of patients with breast cancer experience bone metastasis. C-X-C motif chemokine ligand 12 (CXCL12) is a chemokine, which plays a crucial role in bone metastasis. … The results revealed that the levels of CXCL12 were significantly increased in patients with breast cancer experiencing bone metastasis. … Furthermore, CXCL12 exhibited a higher diagnostic accuracy (area under the curve [AUC], 0.7940; P = 0.0008) at a cut-off value of >1,392 pg/mL compared with CA15.3.”
