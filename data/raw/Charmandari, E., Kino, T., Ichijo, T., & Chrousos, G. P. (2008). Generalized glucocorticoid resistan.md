# Charmandari, E., Kino, T., Ichijo, T., & Chrousos, G. P. (2008). Generalized glucocorticoid resistance: Clinical aspects, molecular mechanisms, and implications of a rare genetic disorder. The Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism, 93(5), 1563–1572.

## Page /

“Primary generalized glucocorticoid resistance (Chrousos syndrome) is a rare genetic disorder characterized by impaired sensitivity of all target tissues to glucocorticoids. This syndrome results from heterozygous, loss‐of‐function mutations in the human glucocorticoid receptor (hGR) gene (NR3C1) located on chromosome 5q31–32. To date, over 30 different NR3C1 mutations—most of which disrupt ligand binding or DNA‐binding functions of the receptor—have been identified in affected patients. These inactivating NR3C1 mutations reduce feedback inhibition at the level of the hypothalamus and pituitary, leading to compensatory increases in ACTH secretion and resultant elevations in adrenal steroid production.”
