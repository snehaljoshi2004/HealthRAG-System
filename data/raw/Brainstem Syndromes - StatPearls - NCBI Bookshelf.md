# Brainstem Syndromes - StatPearls - NCBI Bookshelf.

## Section

Lesions within the brainstem, particularly in the pons, commonly produce crossed syndromes. These are characterized by an ipsilateral (same-sided) cranial nerve deficit (such as a complete facial palsy due to involvement of the facial nerve nucleus or its fibers) and a contralateral (opposite-sided) hemiparesis or hemiplegia due to disruption of the descending corticospinal tracts before their decussation in the medulla
