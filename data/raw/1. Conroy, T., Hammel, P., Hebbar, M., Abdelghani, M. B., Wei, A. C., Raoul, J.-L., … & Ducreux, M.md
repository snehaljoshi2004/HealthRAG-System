“The PRODIGE 24/CCTG PA.6 randomized trial in resected pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma demonstrated that adjuvant modified FOLFIRINOX (oxaliplatin 85 mg/m² IV Day 1; leucovorin 400 mg/m² IV Day 1; irinotecan 150 mg/m² IV Day 1; 5-fluorouracil 2400 mg/m² infusion over 46 h Day 1, every two weeks for 12 cycles) significantly improved disease-free and overall survival compared to gemcitabine, establishing mFOLFIRINOX as the preferred adjuvant regimen in fit patients with negative margins, low postoperative CA 19-9, and high-risk features such as perineural invasion.” 
“Clinically, perineural invasion is a potential indication for adjuvant radiotherapy and is considered in staging and risk stratification of pancreatic adenocarcinoma.”
