# Purves, D., Augustine, G. J., Fitzpatrick, D., Katz, L. C., LaMantia, A. S., McNamara, J. O., & Williams, S. M. (Eds.). (2001). Neuroscience (2nd ed.). Sinauer Associates

## Section

Large, myelinated afferents (Aβ fibers), which are thick, myelinated sensory fibers, are responsible for transmitting information about touch, pressure, vibration, and proprioception. Their large diameter and myelination allow for rapid conduction of these precise sensory modalities.
