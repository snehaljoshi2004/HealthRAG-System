# Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) - StatPearls - NCBI Bookshelf

## Section

The simultaneous presence of upper motor neuron (UMN) signs, such as a positive Babinski sign and brisk reflexes, alongside lower motor neuron (LMN) signs, including muscle atrophy and fasciculations, in the same limb or body region is the hallmark of a disease affecting both motor neuron systems. This clinical picture is most characteristic of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS).
